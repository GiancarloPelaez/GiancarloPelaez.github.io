About

I am a Master of Science candidate in Development Economics at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, with prior degrees in Economics and Political Science from Pontificia Universidad Javeriana in Bogotá.

My work sits at the intersection of applied microeconometrics and development policy. I am currently researching the causal impact of remittances on financial development in Colombia, using longitudinal household data and a shift-share instrumental-variables strategy. Earlier, I conducted qualitative fieldwork on indigenous governance and rural political economy in post-conflict Colombia.

Alongside research, I bring several years of industry experience in data analytics and operations at NielsenIQ and Schlumberger.

Research

Remittances and Financial Development in Colombia
Master's thesis · Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne · 2025–present
  • Estimating the causal effect of remittances on financial inclusion using large longitudinal household data.
  • Identification via heterogeneous-effects 2SLS with a shift-share (Bartik) instrument strategy.
  • Aims to generate policy-relevant findings on financial access in a developing-economy setting.
